# Smoked Pork Butt

**Prep time:** 30 mins | **Smoke time:** 90 mins-2 hrs per lb | **Temp:** 225°F-250°F

### Ingredients

- 1 Pork butt or pork shoulder, 5-8 lb
- 2-3 Tbsp Yellow mustard
- 1/3-1/2 cup BBQ rub
- _Optional spritz:_ Apple juice, apple cider vinegar, or a 50/50 mix
- _Optional wrap:_ Foil or butcher paper
- _Optional finish:_ BBQ sauce, extra rub, or reserved juices

### Instructions

1. **Trim:** Trim any hard fat or loose flaps. Leave some fat on the pork to protect it during the long cook.
2. **Bind & Season:** Coat the pork with a thin layer of mustard, then season generously on all sides with BBQ rub.
3. **Smoke:** Preheat the smoker to 225°F-250°F. Smoke until the bark is set and the internal temperature reaches the stall, usually 145°F-165°F.
4. **Wrap (Optional):** Wrap in foil or butcher paper once the bark looks good. Add a small splash of spritz liquid if you want extra moisture.
5. **Finish:** Continue cooking until the pork reaches 195°F-201°F and pulls apart easily.
6. **Rest:** Rest wrapped for at least 45 minutes, or up to 2 hours in a cooler.
7. **Pull & Season:** Shred the pork, discard large pieces of fat, then mix in extra seasoning, sauce, or juices to taste.
