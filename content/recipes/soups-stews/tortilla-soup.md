# Tortilla Soup

### Ingredients

- 1 package corn tortillas, preferably white corn; flour tortillas can also be used
- Oil, Crisco or peanut oil seems to work better
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 stalk celery, sliced about 1/4 inch thick
- 2 tomatoes, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
- 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more if needed
- 1/2 teaspoon pepper, plus more if needed; coarse ground preferred
- 2 cloves garlic or equivalent, chopped
- Grated jack cheese
- 1/2 cup picante sauce
- Other cupboard spices that sound good
- 1 chicken
- White rice, optional

### Instructions

1. Place chicken in a large pot of water and cook until meat can be easily pulled from the bone.
2. Remove chicken from the pot and let it cool. Leave broth in the pot.
3. Pull meat from bones and tear or cut into about 1/2 inch cubes.
4. While chicken is cooking and cooling, heat about 2 tablespoons oil in another pot large enough to cook the vegetables.
5. When oil is hot, add celery, tomatoes, onion, and garlic.
6. Similar to stir fry, cook vegetables until just tender, stirring frequently.
7. Return chicken to the pot with vegetables, add picante sauce, and add broth so it is 1 or 2 inches above the veggie mix. If serving over rice, more broth is better.
8. Add water if needed to make enough liquid.
9. Cook over moderate heat, a good simmer, for about 45 minutes.
10. Check flavor and add salt and pepper to taste. Lawry's seasoned salt can be used in place of regular salt.

### Tortilla Chips

1. While soup is cooking, heat oil hot enough that tortillas sizzle when dropped in, similar to frying french fries.
2. Cook only a handful at a time.
3. Remove tortillas when they just start to brown. They will continue to brown after being removed from the pan.
4. Drain on a paper towel and salt lightly.
5. Start the next handful.
6. The tortillas should brown quickly, in about a minute.

### Serving

1. Place a small handful of cheese in the bottom of a bowl and pour the soup over it.
2. If the soup is not hot enough, the cheese gets really stringy.
3. Tortilla chips can be eaten on the side like crackers or dumped in the bowl.
4. Serve with or without white rice.

### Notes

- The scan says the tortillas are often eaten before the soup is done if too much time passes.
