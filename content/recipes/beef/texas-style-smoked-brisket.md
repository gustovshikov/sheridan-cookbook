# Texas-Style Smoked Brisket

**Prep time:** 45 mins | **Smoke time:** 10-16 hrs + 2-6 hrs rest | **Temp:** 225°F

### Ingredients

- 1 Whole packer brisket, 10-14 lb
- 2 Tbsp Kosher salt
- 2 Tbsp Coarse black pepper
- _Optional:_ 1 Tbsp Garlic powder
- _Optional binder:_ Yellow mustard or hot sauce
- _Optional wrap:_ Butcher paper or foil
- _Optional:_ Beef tallow or beef broth for wrapping

### Instructions

1. **Trim:** Trim hard fat and silverskin from the brisket. Leave about 1/4 inch of fat on the fat cap and square off thin edges so they do not overcook.
2. **Season:** Apply a thin binder, if using. Mix the salt, pepper, and optional garlic powder, then season all sides heavily. Rest at room temperature for 30 to 45 minutes while the smoker heats.
3. **Smoke:** Preheat the smoker to 225°F. Smoke the brisket fat-side toward the heat until the bark is dark and the internal temperature is around 165°F.
4. **Wrap (Optional):** Wrap tightly in butcher paper or foil with a little beef tallow or broth if the bark is set and the brisket has entered the stall.
5. **Finish:** Continue cooking until the thickest part reaches 200°F-205°F and a probe slides in with very little resistance.
6. **Rest (2-6 hrs):** Keep the brisket wrapped and rest it in a cooler or warm oven. A longer rest makes slicing easier and keeps it juicy.
7. **Slice:** Slice the flat against the grain. Turn the point and slice it separately, also against the grain.
