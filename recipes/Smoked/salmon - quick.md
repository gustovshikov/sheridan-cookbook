# Quick Hot-Smoked Salmon Portions

**Prep time:** ~1 hr 15 mins | **Smoke time:** 45–60 mins | **Temp:** 225°F–250°F

### Ingredients

- 1 Thawed salmon fillet (skin-on)
- ½ cup Kosher salt
- ½ cup Dark brown sugar
- _Optional:_ Black pepper, garlic powder, or smoked paprika
- _Optional glaze:_ Maple syrup or honey

### Instructions

1. **Portion:** Cut the salmon fillet into uniform 1.5 to 2-inch wide strips, keeping the skin on.
2. **Flash Cure (30–45 mins):** Mix the salt, brown sugar, and any optional spices. Pack the mixture generously over the flesh side of the salmon in a baking dish. Let sit at room temperature for 30 to 45 minutes until a wet syrup forms.
3. **Rinse & Dry:** Thoroughly rinse all of the cure off the salmon under cold water. Pat completely dry with paper towels.
4. **Force the Pellicle (30 mins):** Place the salmon pieces on a wire cooling rack. Aim a fan directly at the fish on high for 30 minutes to rapidly dry the surface until it feels tacky.
5. **Smoke (45–60 mins):** Preheat the smoker to 225°F–250°F using a light wood (alder, apple, cherry). Place the salmon skin-side down on the grates.
6. **Finish:** Smoke until the internal temperature reaches 145°F or the flesh flakes easily. _(Optional: Brush with maple syrup or honey during the last 15 minutes)._
