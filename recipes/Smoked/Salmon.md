# Hot-Smoked Salmon Fillet

**Prep time:** 1 hr 30 mins to overnight | **Smoke time:** 1-2 hrs | **Temp:** 225°F

### Ingredients

- 1 Salmon fillet, skin-on
- 1/2 cup Dark brown sugar
- 1/4 cup Kosher salt
- _Optional:_ 1 tsp Garlic powder
- _Optional:_ Black pepper or smoked paprika
- _Optional glaze:_ Honey or maple syrup mixed with a little brown sugar

### Instructions

1. **Prep:** Remove any pin bones and pat the salmon dry. Leave the skin on and keep the fillet whole or cut it into large portions.
2. **Dry Brine (1-8 hrs):** Mix the brown sugar, kosher salt, and any optional seasonings. Coat the flesh side of the salmon well. For a shorter cure, use a salt-heavier mix and brine for about 40 to 60 minutes; for a gentler cure, use the sweeter mix above and brine up to 8 hours in the fridge.
3. **Rinse & Dry:** Rinse the brine off under cold water, then pat the salmon completely dry.
4. **Form the Pellicle (30-60 mins):** Place the salmon on a wire rack, uncovered, until the surface feels tacky. Use the fridge or a fan to speed this up.
5. **Glaze:** Brush the flesh side lightly with honey or maple syrup and a sprinkle of brown sugar, if using.
6. **Smoke (1-2 hrs):** Preheat the smoker to 225°F using a mild wood like alder, apple, or cherry. Smoke the salmon skin-side down.
7. **Finish:** Pull the salmon when the thickest part reaches 135°F-140°F internal and flakes easily. Rest 5 minutes before serving.
